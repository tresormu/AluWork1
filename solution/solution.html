Our Solutions

Why We Exist

Our Mission

Our mission is to solve Uganda's youth unemployment crisis — because job creation is not just an economic issue, it is a matter of national survival. A country that cannot employ its youth cannot grow, cannot stabilize, and cannot develop. Through bold research and community-driven advocacy, we work to create real jobs and restore dignity.

The Challenge

Problem Statement

In Kiruhura, Uganda, thousands of young people wake up every day with energy and ambition — but nowhere to direct them. Nationally, 16.1% of young Ugandans are unemployed, and 41% of youth aged 18–30 are not in employment, education, or training. Young people are not lazy — they are trapped.

What We Propose

Five Pillars of Change

01

Agricultural Training

Mobile centers teaching agro-processing (milk, coffee, fruit) with paid internships and direct partnerships with local cooperatives.

02

Financing & Equipment

A micro-credit fund combined with shared equipment rental to lower the entry barrier for young entrepreneurs in the region.

03

Support for Young Women

Childcare during training sessions paired with structured mentorship from established local women entrepreneurs.

04

Job Creation

Local processing plants to create formal employment with direct links to market buyers and regional supply chains.

05

Job Information Platform

An SMS/WhatsApp system listing local job and training opportunities — accessible even on basic feature phones.

The Vision

A Future Worth Building

We envision a Kiruhura where every young person — regardless of gender, background, or location — has access to a dignified livelihood. Not through charity, but through systemic change: skills, capital, networks, and opportunity. That is the future we are working toward.
